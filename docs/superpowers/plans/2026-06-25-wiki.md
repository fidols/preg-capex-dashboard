## 3. CapEx Domain Vocabulary

| Term | Definition | Why It Matters |
|------|-----------|----------------|
| **CapEx** | Capital expenditures — money spent improving or upgrading properties (vs. OpEx, which is ongoing operations) | Everything in this role |
| **Change Order** | A contractor request to modify the original contract scope and price — adds cost, time, or both | Major budget risk; must be tracked and approved carefully |
| **Pay Application (Pay App)** | Monthly invoice contractors submit for work completed — reviewed against contract schedule of values | How you know money is leaving the account |
| **Cost-to-Complete (CTC)** | Estimated remaining spend to finish a project | Key forecast metric Adam will ask about |
| **Budget-to-Actual Variance** | Gap between planned spend and actual spend, expressed as $ and % | The core of weekly reporting |
| **Committed Costs** | Costs contractually obligated (signed contracts + approved change orders) but not yet paid | Important for forecasting — it's real money, just not spent yet |
| **Schedule of Values (SOV)** | Breakdown of a construction contract by line item (foundation, framing, MEP, etc.) | How pay apps are evaluated |
| **Retainage** | % of each pay app withheld until project completion — typically 5–10% | Shows up as unpaid liability until final |
| **NOI** | Net Operating Income — property revenue minus operating expenses | How investors evaluate an asset's value |
| **Cap Rate** | NOI ÷ Property Value — used to price acquisitions | Lower cap rate = higher value (more expensive market) |
| **Value-Add** | Strategy of buying underperforming properties, doing CapEx, raising NOI | PREG's core playbook |
| **LIHTC** | Low Income Housing Tax Credit — federal program that finances affordable housing via tax credits sold to investors | PREG has a meaningful affordable housing portfolio |
| **Multifamily** | Apartment communities (5+ units) — vs. single-family homes | PREG's primary asset class |
| **Procore** | Construction project management software — where budgets, change orders, submittals, RFIs, and pay apps live | You'll pull data from here daily |
| **Yardi** | Real estate ERP — property accounting, leases, AP, GL | Source of truth for financial data |
| **Entrata** | Multifamily property management platform — leasing, resident portals, rent collection | More on the operations side |
| **Vertically Integrated** | Company controls multiple steps of the value chain in-house (construction + PM + services) | PREG's key differentiator |
| **GC (General Contractor)** | The main contractor managing a construction project — Post Construction is PREG's own GC | Internal accountability, not arms-length |
| **Subcontractor** | Specialty trades hired by the GC (electrical, plumbing, HVAC, etc.) | Their pay apps flow through Procore |
```

## 4. Procore and Yardi — Conceptual Workflows

*Note: No direct experience with either yet. These are the conceptual workflows — the data logic is what transfers from Python/Excel experience.*

### Procore
Procore is where construction projects live. The Financial Analyst uses it to:
- **View project budgets** — original contract amounts broken down by cost code
- **Track change orders** — each CO has a status (pending, approved, rejected); approved COs increase the committed cost
- **Review pay applications** — contractors submit monthly; Procore routes them for approval
- **Pull cost reports** — budget vs. actual by cost code, by project, or across portfolio

**Key mental model:** Procore is the *construction source of truth*. Numbers here reflect what contractors say is happening on the ground.

### Yardi
Yardi is the financial ERP. The Financial Analyst uses it to:
- **Verify actual payments** — AP (accounts payable) entries confirm money left the bank
- **Code expenses** — CapEx transactions get coded to the right property, cost center, and GL account
- **Pull financial reports** — trial balance, GL detail, project cost reports
- **Support month-end close** — CapEx accruals, reconciliation against Procore

**Key mental model:** Yardi is the *accounting source of truth*. Numbers here are what actually happened financially.

### Procore ↔ Yardi Reconciliation
A core part of the analyst job is reconciling these two:
- Procore shows a pay app was approved for $250K
- Yardi should show a $250K payment posted to that property's CapEx account
- If they don't match → investigate (timing issue? coding error? unapproved payment?)

### Entrata
Used more by the property management side. May surface in reporting (e.g., tracking renovation-to-lease-up timelines — how long after a unit CapEx project does it get re-leased and at what rent?).

### Excel
Despite Procore and Yardi, heavy Excel use is expected for:
- CapEx forecast models (budget + committed + remaining by project)
- Change order log (running tracker of all COs, status, $ impact)
- Portfolio-level CapEx summary for leadership
- Scenario planning ("what if we defer 3 projects to Q3?")
```

## 5. LIHTC and Affordable Housing Finance Basics

### What is LIHTC?
The **Low Income Housing Tax Credit** is the primary federal tool for financing affordable rental housing. Created by the Tax Reform Act of 1986 (Section 42 of the tax code).

### How It Works (Simple Version)
1. A developer proposes an affordable housing project
2. State housing agency allocates tax credits to the project
3. Developer sells those tax credits to investors (usually banks) for cash upfront — typically ~$0.90–$0.95 per $1 of credit
4. That cash becomes the equity in the deal, reducing the debt the project needs to carry
5. In exchange, the property must keep rents affordable for income-qualified tenants for 15–30 years

### Two Types of Credits
- **9% credits** — competitive, larger subsidy, used for new construction and substantial rehabilitation
- **4% credits** — non-competitive (available to all qualifying projects), used with tax-exempt bond financing, smaller subsidy

### What This Means for CapEx at PREG
- LIHTC properties have **compliance requirements** — CapEx work must preserve the affordability covenant
- **Rent restrictions** limit NOI, which makes investors more sensitive to CapEx overruns (less margin for error)
- **Regulatory agreements** (the legal compliance documents) may govern which improvements require approval
- **Year 15 exit** — LIHTC partnerships often have a buyout or exit event around year 15 when the compliance period ends

### GIS / Community Investment Angle
PREG focuses affordable housing in communities with genuine need. The spatial analysis work (food desert mapping, underserved community identification) is adjacent to how affordable housing developers think about where to deploy capital — this is a natural bridge from the LMU GIS project.
