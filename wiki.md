### What Makes PRG Vertically Integrated

The services page describes an integrated stack — they don't just invest; they manage the asset, execute the work, and advise on strategy. Verticals as listed on the website:

| Business Line | What It Does |
|---|---|
| **Property Management** | Full-service PM including leasing, tenant screening, maintenance, 24/7 on-call, tenant portal (AppFolio). Manages conventional, lease-up, high-rise, garden, mixed-use, and commercial. |
| **Asset Management** | Strategic planning, investment, and financing oversight for clients and their own portfolio. Principals have "executed and overseen a combined billions of dollars in acquisitions, land use, and dispositions." |
| **Investment & Development** | Private equity and debt strategies; value-add repositioning of mismanaged or capital-starved assets. Accredited investor and JV opportunities offered via **Rumah Capital LLC** (investment affiliate — website currently offline). |
| **Sales** | Acquisitions, dispositions, and land use work across residential, mixed-use, and commercial. |
| **Strategy & Consulting** | Custom strategy for private and public sector clients: market research, brand positioning, alignment with real estate markets. |

**Why this matters for CapEx:** Vertical integration means when you track a budget variance, the construction arm and the property management team are both internal — you are not reconciling against an outside contractor's ledger. Change orders affect in-house teams. This is a different accountability dynamic than managing third-party GCs.

> **Note:** The website does NOT name subsidiary companies called "Post Construction" or "Post Property Management" as distinct legal entities, and does NOT mention title or insurance subsidiaries. These names appear in the planning documents but were not confirmed on the website as of the scrape date (June 25, 2026). Clarify at the interview which verticals are in-house vs. affiliated.

### Key Signals for the Interview

1. **Boutique, not institutional** — PRG is a privately held, growth-stage firm, not a public REIT or institutional operator at Greystar's scale. Adam came from massive-scale operations; PREG is building toward that. Your role will likely involve building or improving systems, not just running them.

2. **Inc. 5000 recognition (2022)** — top 10% fastest-growing private company in the US by revenue. This is a firm that was growing fast three years ago. Come prepared to ask about the current growth trajectory.

3. **Value-add is the playbook** — CapEx isn't maintenance, it's a strategic investment tool. Every dollar of CapEx should show up in higher rents, better occupancy, or a better exit valuation.

4. **California + Florida geographic split** — the California portfolio is high-end residential in the LA metro; Florida is university-adjacent (Tallahassee) at more modest rents. These are very different CapEx profiles — ask how reporting is structured across markets.

5. **AppFolio is confirmed, Procore/Yardi are not** — don't claim familiarity with their specific tech stack beyond AppFolio. Say you understand the category of tools.
