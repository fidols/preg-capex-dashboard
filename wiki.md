### What Makes PREG Unique

PREG is **vertically integrated** — they don't just invest in real estate, they control every step under the Post Investment Group umbrella:

| Division | Entity | What It Does |
|---|---|---|
| **Construction Management** | 1st Call (my1stcall.com) | General contracting and construction management throughout a property's lifecycle — this is the internal GC that executes CapEx work |
| **Property Management** | TAM Residential (tamresidential.com) | Full-service PM maximizing value of multifamily assets — the internal PM company that runs the assets |
| **Insurance & Risk Services** | Maple Street Insurance (maplestreetinsurance.com) | Comprehensive insurance coverage with multifamily expertise across the entire ownership lifecycle |
| **Title Services** | Sunset Crest Title (sunsetcresttitle.com) | Full suite of title services with deep affordable housing regulatory expertise — critical for LIHTC transactions |

**Why this matters for CapEx:** A budget overrun or change order touches their own construction arm (1st Call), their own PM team (TAM Residential), and their own accounting systems — all in-house. You are not reconciling against a third-party GC's ledger. This is a fundamentally different accountability structure than managing external contractors.

PREG is also a **preferred borrower and select sponsor of Fannie Mae and Freddie Mac**, which means they can secure superior financing terms and have closed more than 100 agency loans. Their reputation is built on closing quickly, navigating complexity, and executing with precision — relevant for CapEx because capital planning is tightly linked to refinancing and exit timing.

### Investment Thesis / Strategy

PREG describes itself as the "industry leader in multifamily and affordable housing." Core strategy elements:

- **High-barrier, high-impact opportunities** — off-market and complex assets that others can't easily execute on. PREG was among the first firms to systematically pursue complex LIHTC transactions at scale.
- **Three core plays:**
  1. **Public/Private Joint Ventures** — partnerships with local government agencies and non-profits to create long-term affordability and favorable tax frameworks. PREG pioneered proprietary JV structures in this space.
  2. **Affordable Housing (LIHTC)** — rent-restricted assets offer built-in downside protection and long-term demand resiliency, insulating investors from economic downturns.
  3. **Bond Financing** — federally tax-exempt municipal bonds, including non-traditional tax-exempt bond structures (see Paddock Club Columbia case study).
- **Distressed asset turnarounds** — proven track record in repositioning under-performing assets.
- **Value add** — CapEx-driven repositioning to raise NOI and improve community quality.
- **Community impact** — strong social mission: financial counseling, education, health/wellness, legal assistance programs on-site; allocates ~$20K/month to social services. Partners with non-profits including Anti-Recidivism Coalition, World Relief, Catholic Charities, Upbring, Crossroads Housing, Refugee Services of Texas.
- **Fannie/Freddie preferred status** — competitive financing advantage; 100+ agency loans closed.

### Key Signals for the Interview

1. **This is a heavily affordable-housing-weighted operator** — only 3.16% of the current portfolio is straight market-rate. PREG is first and foremost an affordable/workforce housing specialist, not a conventional value-add apartment company. CapEx here intersects with LIHTC compliance timelines, regulatory oversight, and bond covenants — not just punch lists and rent bumps.

2. **Vertically integrated = internal accountability** — change orders go to their own construction arm (1st Call), approved by their own PM company (TAM Residential), and tracked in their own systems. Ask how CapEx budgets are structured between the RE entity and the construction subsidiary.

3. **Technology stack not confirmed publicly** — the planning document referenced Procore, Yardi, and Entrata. These were not confirmed on the website. TAM Residential (the PM subsidiary) likely has its own software stack. Confirm the CapEx tracking and PM software in the interview before claiming familiarity.

4. **Scale has grown significantly** — the company went from ~$3.5B to $6.3B in current portfolio value. They are still acquiring at $1.5–2B/year. This is a firm in active growth mode, which means CapEx work is expanding. Come prepared to discuss how you scale financial reporting and budget tracking across a growing portfolio.

5. **Jason Post is the founder** — this is a founder-led company. The culture will reflect that: entrepreneurial, fast-moving, but with the complexity of 1,250+ employees and institutional-scale compliance requirements. Expect a mix of institutional process and startup agility.

6. **Post Investment Group is the parent** — PREG is one of 9 operating companies under Post Investment Group. The other companies (The Now Massage, Hawks Kids, Bowie Barker, 710 Labs) are unrelated to real estate. This context matters: the CFO, COO, and legal/people functions are shared across the holding company, not purely real estate-focused.
